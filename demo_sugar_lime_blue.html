From the Editor

Welcome to Issue #1 of the Sugar Lime Blue artist edition. This is the first of our artist-specific dispatches, and we picked Sugar Lime Blue on purpose: a Nashville band whose history reads like a working musician's case study in the value of slow growth, real partnerships, and refusing to give up after a hard loss. Inside this issue you'll find the band's verified history, current lineup, full discography, and where to catch them live. Every fact comes with a source link.

— Sarah Collins, Editor

WHO THEY ARE

Sugar Lime Blue are an Americana / blues / folk / country / jam group based in Nashville, Tennessee. The band self-describes their style as “Cosmic Cowgirl” — a phrase that does more useful work than most genre tags, because it tells you to expect Grateful-Dead-shaped patience underneath blues phrasing and country instrumentation.

The band's roots go back to 1999, when guitarist Dave Beth and vocalist Ashley Beth first met in Texas. Ashley had been asked to add background vocals to Dave's earlier project. They married, kept writing together, and gave Sugar Lime Blue its first official shows in 2007. The band name itself was Dave's coinage at home; in Ashley's preferred description: “Sugar, the sweet stuff; Lime, the citrus fruit; Blue, the color.” Sweet, sour, and bluesy.

THE LINEUP, NOW

The current Sugar Lime Blue is a four-piece:

  • Ashley Beth — lead vocals. Co-founder.
  • Dave Beth — lead guitar. Co-founder.
  • Matt Fox — bass.
  • Ikaika Pekelo — drums and percussion.

Two members of the band's earlier eras deserve naming. Bassist Russ Dean joined around 2010, after relocating to the Nashville area from California, and the trio that formed around the Beths leaned hard on a shared love of jam-band music — the Grateful Dead in particular. Russ Dean passed away in 2020. After that loss, Joe Bass (also performing as Brian Howe) carried the bass chair on tour through the band's interim period.

That history matters because Sugar Lime Blue's catalogue is shaped by it. The records before and after 2020 are the same band, but they are not the same year of that band's life.

THE DISCOGRAPHY, ON THE RECORD

Four albums, recorded across two decades and two studios — one their own, one one of Nashville's most storied rooms.

Far From The Tree (2011) — Debut album. Released independently.

Move That Earth — Sophomore album. Released independently. (We do not yet have a verified release year on file; we'll add it in Issue #2.)

Narcoluptuous — The band's third studio album, 12 tracks, recorded at Bluebird, Sugar Lime Blue's home studio just outside Nashville. Released independently. (Year not yet re-verified.)

The Blackbird Sessions (2022) — The latest release: 9 tracks, recorded at the iconic Blackbird Studio in Nashville. Indie Band Guru highlighted “Burn It” and “Didn't Mean To Kill You” as standouts; the album was picked up by more than 100 FM radio stations on release. The record arrives on the other side of Russ Dean's passing, which gives some of its quieter moments their full weight.

WHAT WE DIDN'T INCLUDE, AND WHY

A note about the things this issue does not say.

The release year for Move That Earth and Narcoluptuous is not on file because we have not re-verified those dates. We chose to leave the year blank rather than guess. Same goes for the labels behind the album releases — the band has operated independently across the catalogue, but if a release was distributed through a third-party imprint, we want a source before we name one.

We also do not name a touring booker, a press contact, or a current management team in Issue #1. Those relationships change. We'll add them as we confirm them.

If you have current information on any of the above — a release year, a credit, an upcoming show — reply to this email with a source link. We update the verified facts sheet weekly.

🎧 Trivia Answer

B) Blackbird Studio in Nashville

The Blackbird Sessions (2022) was tracked at the iconic Blackbird Studio in Nashville — nine songs, picked up by 100+ FM radio stations on release. The band's home studio “Bluebird,” just outside Nashville, was the room behind the earlier Narcoluptuous record. Both spellings are easy to confuse and we have done it ourselves.
